Ashraf Mohamed

Autobiography

I was born in Chicago on February 18, 1997. I am currently a student intern at Year up Chicago and I am also attending college full time to gain my bachelor’s degree in computer engineering. Ever since I was a kid I always had a knack for technology. While growing up I was always an honor student, until I had to switch schools to get a better education. Going to a new high school gave me a new insight on how schools have different educational systems. The grading system was tougher, the teaching style, and the academic programs were different than what I was used to. These changes made me become who I am today. In high school I always knew that I wanted to go for a bachelor’s degree in computer engineering. Which is the reason why I enrolled in Year Up, so that I could gain better knowledge and experience in IT. I also didn’t want to be behind in my college education, so I applied to Ivy Tech Community College for my first year. In the future I plan on going to a University to finish the necessary courses to get my degree. Along the way I’m hoping to begin my career in IT with the help of the internship, which is giving me the opportunity to learn how the business world works and helping me build my brand.
